Professional Thesis

I am based in New York City, originally from Bangladesh. My work sits at the intersection of cybersecurity, digital forensics, artificial intelligence, and applied data systems. I fundamentally believe that high-quality technology should solve structural problems while preserving human privacy.

I currently serve as a Data Analyst at the City University of New York (CUNY) Central Office and an Adjunct Lecturer in Computer Science at John Jay College. In the classroom, I prioritize systems-thinking and ethical computing. In the office, I build deterministic reconciliation workflows and robust validation pipelines handling massive, multi-campus datasets.

Beyond the terminal, I am an avid traveler and photographer. Experiencing diverse cultures continues to shape how I approach system architecture: with empathy, wide perspective, and an eye for structural resilience.
